# Kitchen Hygiene Self-Audit Checklist v1.0

A standardized checklist for performing internal kitchen hygiene audits, based on HACCP and GMP principles. Use this weekly to ensure compliance and identify areas for improvement.

**Instructions:** For each question, check the box if the condition is met. If any boxes are left unchecked, note corrective actions needed.

## 1. Personal Hygiene

- [ ] All staff are wearing clean and appropriate uniforms (including head coverings if required)?
- [ ] Hands are being washed thoroughly at designated hand-washing stations before starting work, after handling raw food, after using the restroom, and after handling waste?
- [ ] No jewelry is being worn on hands or arms (except a plain wedding band)?
- [ ] Staff with open wounds, cuts, or sores have them covered with waterproof, brightly colored bandages?
- [ ] No staff member is working while experiencing symptoms of illness (vomiting, diarrhea, fever)?
- [ ] Hair restraints (nets/hats) are being worn by all kitchen staff?
- [ ] Fingernails are kept short, clean, and free of polish or artificial nails?
- [ ] Disposable gloves are changed between tasks and when torn or soiled?

## 2. Receiving & Storage

- [ ] All incoming food deliveries are checked for temperature compliance (chilled foods ≤5°C, frozen foods ≤-18°C)?
- [ ] Packaging is intact, undamaged, and free from signs of pest infestation?
- [ ] Delivery vehicles are clean and appropriately refrigerated where required?
- [ ] Food items are immediately moved to appropriate storage areas (dry, chilled, frozen) after delivery?
- [ ] Storage areas are clean, dry, well-ventilated, and free from pests?
- [ ] Food is stored using the "First In, First Out" (FIFO) method, with clear date labeling?
- [ ] Raw and ready-to-eat foods are stored separately to avoid cross-contamination (raw below cooked)?

## 3. Preparation & Cooking

- [ ] Separate color-coded cutting boards and utensils are used for raw meat, poultry, seafood, vegetables, and ready-to-eat foods?
- [ ] Food contact surfaces are cleaned and sanitized between different tasks?
- [ ] Thermometers are calibrated and used to check internal cooking temperatures (poultry ≥74°C, ground meat ≥71°C, fish ≥63°C)?
- [ ] Food is not left in the Temperature Danger Zone (5°C–60°C) for more than 2 hours total?
- [ ] Thawing is done under refrigeration, under cold running water, or as part of the cooking process (never at room temperature)?
- [ ] Leftovers are cooled rapidly (from 60°C to 21°C within 2 hours, and to 5°C within a further 4 hours)?
- [ ] Taste-testing is done with disposable utensils (no double-dipping)?

## 4. Cleaning & Sanitizing

- [ ] Cleaning schedules are posted and followed for all equipment and areas?
- [ ] Three-compartment sink (or equivalent) is set up correctly: wash, rinse, sanitize?
- [ ] Sanitizer concentration is checked with test strips and is within the recommended range (chlorine 50–100 ppm, quat 200–400 ppm)?
- [ ] Dishwashing machines are reaching the required final rinse temperature (≥82°C for hot sanitizing) or chemical sanitizer concentration?
- [ ] All cleaning chemicals are stored separately from food, utensils, and packaging?
- [ ] Cleaning cloths are color-coded (e.g., red for restrooms, blue for general surfaces, green for food contact) and laundered/disinfected daily?
- [ ] Waste bins are lined, covered, and emptied regularly (at least daily)?

## 5. Pest Control

- [ ] External doors and windows are kept closed or fitted with insect screens?
- [ ] No gaps or holes in walls, floors, or ceilings where pests could enter?
- [ ] Food is stored in pest-proof containers (sealed lids, metal/glass/rigid plastic)?
- [ ] No standing water or food debris is present in drain traps, floor corners, or equipment crevices?
- [ ] Pest monitoring devices (traps, bait stations) are in place, checked regularly, and records kept?
- [ ] External areas are free of rubbish, overgrown vegetation, and stagnant water that could attract pests?
- [ ] Pest control service reports are up-to-date and any issues are being addressed?
